"MASSA CONFUSA: ON THE INNER JOURNEY"

2024, mixed media

"Massa Confusa: On the Inner Journey" details the psychological and physical healing journey from a tragic life event, such as a death, loss, or other life crisis. The term massa confusa originates from alchemical philosophy, describing the state of matter in a confusing mass during the Magnum Opus, the process of acquiring the philosopher’s stone or prima materia. This mass confusion of the substance undergoes a process of finding light and clarity from chaos and turmoil, reshaping as an organized structure after it has been washed away from any impurities.

During the Magnum Opus, matter undergoes four stages: nigredo (blackness), albedo (whiteness), citrinitas (yellowness), and rubedo (redness), all of which form the inner journey of healing. Nigredo, also known as the ‘dark night of the soul,’ is the confrontation of the physical and/or psychological shadow– the encounter with the life crisis. Albedo is the undertaking of purification and where insight into the shadow is realized, inflating the ego and unneeded conceptualizations. Citrinitas illuminates light for the matter towards the end of the reshaping, therefore bringing wisdom during the inner journey. Rubedo concludes the inner journey through the rebirth of the matter and the soul, therefore achieving self-wholeness.

Magnum Opus is the fundamental foundation for which the inner journey builds itself from. The inner journey of healing from a life crisis is one that is difficult. It feels as though the only person that can understand what they’re feeling is only themselves. Despite the journey’s uneasiness, with many trips and falls, light and clarity will form for the path ahead, reshaping into an organized structure. Everyone is handed adversity in their lives. No one’s journey is easy. It’s how one handles adversity that makes them unique. That is what "Massa Confusa: On the Inner Journey" stands for.
