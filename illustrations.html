Drawings & Paintings: When discussing art with anyone, they will most likely refer to paintings and drawings, and for good reason. For much of art's history, painting and drawing have been the main mediums for all artists. These mediums have been the cornerstones for progressing and revolutionizing the world of art. I believe that it was the communication between the art styles of different countries that gave the world a broader definition of what art really is. Many people will agree upon the saying that a picture is worth a thousand words, and such is what paintings and drawings heavily expounded upon. Each stroke of a brush, each color from a color palette, and every symbolic element incorporated in a work allows us to connect with people and events in history. The various amount of art styles allow for different interpretations of what happened in history and what an event or person looked like. For example, Ukiyo-e paintings could interpret an event or person differently than classical atelier paintings we are used to seeing, which could bring about different emotions and opinions to the viewers.
