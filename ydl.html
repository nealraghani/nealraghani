Youth Design Leadership: "The San Jose Youth Design Leadership Program introduces young citizens to the power of design in their own communities through workshop style educational sessions, immersive professional practice opportunities, and the experience of taking a project from concept to design with a roadmap for implementation. The program will take place in-person and around the Serpentine Pavilion and be supplemented by some remote video interaction. Over the course of seven weeks, students will explore architecture, public art, urban design, and real estate development while working alongside Studio Gang and Local Color to design and develop a temporary activation project aimed toward enhanced urban connectivity. This engagement strategy provides crucial and reciprocal benefits. On one hand, young people are supported as advocates for change, trusted as decision-makers, and introduced to diverse career opportunities. In return, project teams receive invaluable feedback at critical junctures from voices that are often disenfranchised during the design process. Studio Gang recognizes that designers play an important role in making it possible for young people to not only participate in community development conversations, but also to be empowered to make decisions, supported as advocates for change, and introduced to pathways towards implementing that change themselves in the future." - Studio Gang & Local Color

During the program, Local Color and students met two times a week during a seven week period (September through early November). Each meeting day was a two hour session of discussions, lectures, group demonstrations, design meetings, site visits, presentations, and interactive working sessions. Additionally, we were expected to complete assignments in between meetings that consisted of site planning and sketches of the finished product and inspirations. At the end of the program, students and local color presented the final project to Studio Gang for feedback and intentions of what happens next.

"The aspiration is that the project will activate the project site, celebrate local ingenuity, create a sense of community ownership, and strengthen the connection between the site and the city. The project can then become a permanent part of the site, or it can be moved, disassembled, reconfigured, and/or re-appropriated throughout the city." - Studio Gang & Local Color
