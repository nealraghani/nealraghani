"Autimus Prime"
2023

Autimus Prime, an animated story book divided into three parts, spotlights the artist’s early life with autism. This animated book incorporates significant elements from the artist’s younger years. One of these elements is a whiteboard their elementary school teacher would use to assess kids’ behavior each day. Anytime students remained on good behavior, the kids would stay on the ‘happy side’ of the board. Otherwise, if they did something that displeased the teacher, they would be moved to the ‘okay side’ or the ‘sad side’. While this whiteboard taught them to behave properly in the classroom, it created a dictatorship-like environment that discouraged freedom of expression. The aforementioned story book is divided based on those three sides of the whiteboard.

Another one of these significant elements is what the elementary school teacher called a “birthday book”. Whenever it was someone’s birthday in the class, classmates would write a page about what they liked most about the birthday student. These were memorable because, behind their wild and energetic nature, the classmates created an internal and external community with one other. Said community understood the hardships of autism and produced an unspoken language that brought happiness and belonging. Such is what this book aims to spotlight. Through the title Autimus Prime, the priority is to spotlight the beauty and hardships of autism behind public perception.
